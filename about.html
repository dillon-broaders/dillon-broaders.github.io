About CV

Hello 👋

I am a very recent alumnus of the School of Physics at Trinity College Dublin. In October 2024, I graduated with a Master of Science degree in Quantum Science and Technology. My connection to Trinity goes further back, however, as I also completed my undergraduate degree there in Physics.

Trinity College Dublin, formally known as The College of the Holy and Undivided Trinity of Queen Elizabeth near Dublin, is Ireland's oldest university, founded in 1592 by Queen Elizabeth I. The School of Physics recently celebrated its 300th anniversary and produced Ireland's only Nobel Prize winner in Physics, Ernest Walton. Trinity consistently ranks as Ireland's top university.

My undergraduate studies in Physics at Trinity provided a well-rounded education that combined theoretical knowledge with practical experience. In addition to core topics such as quantum mechanics, condensed matter physics, electromagnetism and thermodynamics, the program included laboratory work and exposure to chemistry in the first two years, which ultimately deepened my understanding of the physical world from both theoretical and experimental perspectives. It was during my undergraduate studies that I developed a strong interest in computational physics. For my final-year research project, I worked with the Computational Spintronics Group, simulating how disorder affects the magnetic properties of iron lattices (namely the magnetic critical temperature).

During my time as an undergraduate, I was also elected to serve as a student representative on the Board of the School of Physics (the School Executive). This role gave me a deeper connection to the department and allowed me to contribute to discussions on academic and student affairs. Being part of this decision-making process made me feel truly integrated into the School of Physics. I found this to be a rewarding experience.

After completing my undergraduate degree, I decided to pursue a Master's in Quantum Science and Technology. During my Master's, I found that my interests gravitated more toward quantum information theory and open quantum systems. The program provided me with a strong theoretical foundation in quantum science, covering topics such as quantum information science, tensor networks, and quantum materials. It was here that I had the opportunity to collaborate with the Quantum Information Theory Group for my Master's dissertation project, where I explored multi-time Petz recovery for quantum systems with non-negligible memory. I am currently continuing this research as an intern in the group.

Throughout my studies, I also developed strong programming skills, particularly in Python, with over five years of experience. My programming work spans a variety of applications, from data analysis and numerical simulations to quantum computing libraries like Qiskit. I also have experience with optimization tools like CVXPY and some familiarity with Julia. I continue to build on these skills in my current research work.
